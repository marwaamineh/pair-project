Travertines of Pamukkale

30DT

6:30AM-8PM

The primary feature of Pamukkale is the travertine terraces, which are formed from the precipitation of calcium carbonate from the flowing water of the hot springs. The hot springs are fed by a system of underground aquifers that collect rainwater and snowmelt from the surrounding mountains.

Hierapolis Archaeological Museum

60DT

8AM-8:30PM

The Archaeological Museum of Hierapolis is located on an area of 14,000 m2 in the Ancient City of Hierapolis. It is a collection of structures consisting of the Ancient Roman Bath, Gymnasium, and library.

Hierapolis ancient theater

30DT

6:30AM-20:00PM

The theatre at Hierapolis has some of the best-preserved decorative features of any theatre in Turkey. Several decorative friezes from the elaborate scaenae frons have survived intact. One shows Emperor Septimus Severus in procession with his family and the gods, with an inscription and dedication.
